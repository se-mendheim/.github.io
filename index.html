Anyone who owns a camera can say they are a photographer, this term is used to define a wide variety of different individuals so I am going to help describe what it means to me. I seek to find the hidden gems of this world and share them with those around me, I seek to take the beauty in this world and capture it using a machine and that is why I call myself a photographer. The photos that I take are what I find to be inspirational, in a strange sense they tend to be what my definition of beauty is. My whole purpose with my photography is to help others and myself see the world through a different lens *ba dum tss*. But in all seriousness I seek to help others see what I am able to see, the beauty of this creation.

A little backstory on who I am might help.

Although the generational spectrum defines me as a Millennial, I am actually on the cutting edge being part of the Silent Generation. And I think that label more accurately describes me. I am more comfortable behind the lens of a camera than in front of a crowd; I would rather read dystopian fiction in a Starbucks on a Friday night than attend a disco dance at the local club. In full honesty I probably wouldn’t even be at the Starbucks – way to much money for a cup of coffee. My personal favorite places to be, and where I feel at home, is either out in the wilderness exploring creation with a camera or behind a computer practicing my coding skills. Yes I am a programmer, I am currently studying computer science and am in my Junior year (this website in fact is a personal project). When given the opportunity to be outside in nature I will almost always take it. I fell in love with the sounds and smells of the wildlife of the forests. Animals are one of my favorite things to discover and watch.

I began my photography Journey at the age of 14 with a Canon PowerShot (I had a model much earlier than this one, it was much cheaper when I got it). In full honesty looking back that camera was a piece of trash, but it got me hooked, so I can never have enough thanks for it. I got the camera because my family told me that we were taking a vactation to Hawaii and I wanted to get the best shots of this adventure. Mind you this was before every single phone took high quality photos, this was back when you could buy a camera with 25 shots on it from your local CVS. I will admit that taking my first photos with this camera was extremely discouraging, the photos looked terrible and the camera was confusing, every photographer I talked to said that my issue was that I was using a digital camera and not a DSLR, so that didn’t help my discouragement. Looking back now I realize it wasn’t the camera or my abilities, taking a couple of hundred of bad photos is the learning process.

Eventually after practicing a lot with the PowerShot, and I was getting quite good with it, I decided to make the jump to a DSLR. I made this choice due to another trip I was going on outside of the country. I looked around for a long time for the perfect camera, and if you have done this before you know how stupid I probably looked. I had absolutely no idea what ISO, Shutter Speed, Aperture f-Stop meant, so I did what any logical individual human would do when they don’t know something… act like I know it all. After a lot of article reading and speaking with best buy employees I settled on a bundle for the Nikon D3300. Some of you Canon fans out there probably think I’m a traitor for switching from the PowerShot to a Nikon, but for all those out there deciding on which brand to get this is all I have to say, it doesn’t make a difference! I’ll expand on this in the learn page.

Finally, the camera arrived, I was overwhelmed with excitement and confusion. I had no idea how to use this camera. Even though I was confused I still took the camera with me outside the country and practiced a ton. To sum up the rest of my experience with a DSLR I can say that practice and google will help make anyone a better photographer. I have now been shooting for almost 8 years and I adore it. With time and practice photography gets so much better, the photos never stop improving from the previous ones and there are always more stunning things to photograph in this world.
